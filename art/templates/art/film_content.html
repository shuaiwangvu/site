Nice To MEET You

> A Short Film

The pandemic has changed so many aspects of our lives. Many are suffering from social anxiety as society opens up again. It can be difficult for some to regain their lifestyle from before the pandemic. For example, one can find it difficult to come to work or tell jokes in the office as before. Some may find it hard to be on time for appointments. Do you still remember when was the last time you shook someone's hand and looked into his/her eyes saying 'Nice to meet you' ?

Nice to MEET you is a short documentary-like story about Nina. She is a student who suffers from loneliness, procrastination, social anxiety, and the lack of confidence and motivation. For a group assignment, she teamed up with Wang, but both did almost nothing until the last few days. They decide to meet on campus to prevent an empty submission. However, she finds it difficult to pick up the old routine: getting up on time, making the right coffee, putting on makeup, etc. She is not sure if wearing a mask on public transport is still required. In the metro, she is very sensitive to the sound of cough. It's been long since she was on campus, her memory about the buildings is faded and she hesitates about the directions as she rushes to the meeting.

While Nina is a fictional character, many of us could relate to her issues at various levels. The goal of this film is to encourage everyone to regain the courage for social interactions and enjoy being together in person after the pandemic.

The premiere was at the Rialto VU cinema from 7PM on 30th June, 2022.

Thanks for everyone who attended the premiere. The film was voted the winner of Audience Award for the 72 Hour VU Film Challenge.

Costanza Spadafora (as Nina)

Costanza played a student called Nina who suffers from post-pandemic social anxiety. She successfully captured the subtle change of emotions and demonstrated how Nina transformed from lacking confidence to enjoying social interaction again.

Costanza is the co-director and the main actress of this short film. To best match the mood and the scenes, she selected three great pieces of music and played the first one. In addition, she has contributed to the scenes, props, dialogues, editing, etc.

Costanza is a final-year master student of Psychology at the Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam.

Shuai Wang (as Wang)

Shuai is the co-director and the cameraman of this short film. He was also the screenwriter and the lighting designer. He was in charge of editing and the final submission for the competition.

He played the teammate of Nina, a character that brought up the idea to meet. He reduced Nina's social anxiety by lively conversations.

Shuai is a Ph.D. student and a scientific engineer in the computer science department of the Vrije Universiteit Amterdam.
